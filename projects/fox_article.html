About Foxes

Foxes are mammals and belong to the Canidae family. They are omnivores, meaning they eat both plants and animals.

There are twelve speices of "true foxes." These can be found on every continent excluding Antartica. The most common species is the red fox.

Features

Foxes are small to medium in size. They walk on fours legs. They also have a long tail full of fur, and pointed ears.

Habitat

Wild

Foxes are found on six of the seven continents. They are however endangered in many plaes due to destruction of their habitats, and being hunted for their fur.

Urban

Foxes have learned to live with humans in urban environments. They are able to survive on wasted food and do so during the night. They also hunt cats and dogs.
